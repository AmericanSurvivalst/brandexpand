Google Ads for Contractors: Beginner's Guide

Dec 08, 2024 • 7 min read

← Back to Blog

Google Ads can deliver leads fast, but they can also drain your budget if done wrong. Here's how to start right.

Why Google Ads for Contractors

When someone searches "plumber near me" they need help now. Google Ads put you at the top of those searches. Unlike SEO, results are immediate.

Campaign Structure

Start with one Search campaign. Group your ads by service type. Don't mix plumbing and electrical in the same ad group.

Keyword Strategy

Target service + location keywords: "plumber boston," "emergency plumber near me," "drain cleaning service." Use phrase match, not broad match. Add negative keywords to block irrelevant searches.

Writing Ads That Convert

Include your service, location, and a call to action. Mention what makes you different - 24/7 service, same-day appointments, licensed and insured. Use call extensions.

Budget and Bidding

Start with $20-50/day. Use maximize conversions once you have data. Don't spread budget too thin across too many services.

Tracking Setup

You must track conversions - phone calls and form submissions. Without this, you're flying blind. Use Google's call tracking or a third-party solution.

The #1 Mistake

Running ads without tracking. If you don't know which keywords drive calls, you can't optimize. Set up conversion tracking before spending a dollar.
